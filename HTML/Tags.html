Battle of Panipat The Battle of Panipat may refer to the three important battles fought at Panipat, India:

  • First Battle of Panipat (1526), fought between the Mughals under Babur and Ibrahim Lodi (Delhi Sultanate)
  • Second Battle of Panipat (1556), fought between Hemchandra Vikramaditya (Sur Empire) and the Mughals under Akbar
  • Third Battle of Panipat (1761), fought between the Maratha Empire and the Durrani Empire under the Afghan king Ahmad Shah Abdali

The Battle of Panipat was a series of three military conflicts fought in India. The battles took place near the town of Panipat in the state of Haryana. The battles First Battle of Panipat (1526) Babur, the Timurid ruler of Kabulistan, defeated Ibrahim Lodi, the Sultan of Delhi. This battle established the Mughal Empire in India. Second Battle of Panipat (1556) The Mughal army fought against Hemu, a Hindu ruler. Hemu was killed by an arrow, and his army fled. Third Battle of Panipat (1761) The Maratha Empire fought against the Durrani Empire under Ahmad Shah Abdali, the Afghan king. The significance The First Battle of Panipat was one of the earliest battles in India to use gunpowder firearms and field artillery. The battles of Panipat were important events in Indian history. The battles of Panipat were fought over control of Northern India.
